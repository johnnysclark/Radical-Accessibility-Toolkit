# Architectural Context for Accessibility

Domain knowledge for creating accessible architectural graphics and descriptions.

## Drawing Types

### Floor Plans

**What they show**: Horizontal cut through building at ~4 feet height

**Key elements**:
- Walls (solid lines, varying thickness)
- Doors (arcs showing swing direction)
- Windows (parallel lines in wall)
- Stairs (parallel lines with direction arrow)
- Room labels and dimensions
- North arrow and scale

**Tactile priority**: Walls, doors, room labels, circulation paths

### Sections

**What they show**: Vertical cut through building

**Key elements**:
- Ground line
- Floor levels
- Wall construction (often hatched)
- Ceiling/roof profiles
- Vertical dimensions
- Material indications

**Tactile priority**: Floor levels, vertical relationships, key dimensions

### Elevations

**What they show**: Exterior face of building (no perspective)

**Key elements**:
- Building outline
- Window and door openings
- Roof profile
- Ground line
- Material indications
- Vertical dimensions

**Tactile priority**: Overall form, opening locations, proportions

### Site Plans

**What they show**: Bird's eye view of building on land

**Key elements**:
- Building footprint
- Property boundaries
- Circulation (paths, drives)
- Landscape features
- North arrow and scale
- Adjacent context

**Tactile priority**: Building location, entry paths, key landscape

### Details

**What they show**: Enlarged construction assemblies

**Key elements**:
- Material layers
- Connections and joints
- Dimensions (often in mm)
- Material labels
- Section cut indicators

**Tactile priority**: Layer sequence, key connections

## Spatial Concepts

### Orientation

- **North arrow**: Always note orientation in descriptions
- **Entry sequence**: Approach → threshold → interior
- **Wayfinding**: Describe paths of travel

### Scale

- **Human reference**: Door = ~7ft, ceiling = ~9ft
- **Room sizes**: Bathroom ~50sf, bedroom ~120sf, living ~200sf
- **Building scale**: House ~2000sf, apartment ~800sf

### Hierarchy

- **Primary spaces**: Living areas, main circulation
- **Secondary spaces**: Bedrooms, private areas
- **Service spaces**: Bathrooms, kitchens, storage

## Description Framework (Arch-Alt-Text)

### Macro Layer (Overview)
3 sentences covering:
1. Medium and format (floor plan, section, photograph)
2. Subject (what building/space)
3. Purpose (design intent, context)

### Meso Layer (Composition)
4+ sentences covering:
- Overall organization/layout
- Key relationships between spaces
- Orientation and scale
- Materials visible

### Micro Layer (Details)
8+ sentences covering:
- Specific elements and their locations
- Dimensions and proportions
- Textures and material qualities
- Analogies to familiar objects
- Wayfinding cues

## Dimension Formats

### Imperial (US)

- Feet only: `10'`
- Inches only: `6"`
- Combined: `10'-6"` or `10' 6"`

### Metric

- Meters: `3m` or `3.5m`
- Centimeters: `350cm`
- Millimeters: `3500mm` (common in details)

## Architectural Vocabulary

### Structural

- **Column**: Vertical structural support
- **Beam**: Horizontal structural member
- **Load-bearing wall**: Wall supporting structure above
- **Partition**: Non-structural dividing wall

### Circulation

- **Corridor**: Linear passage connecting spaces
- **Vestibule**: Small entry chamber
- **Stair run**: Continuous series of steps
- **Landing**: Platform between stair runs

### Openings

- **Doorway**: Opening for passage
- **Threshold**: Floor at doorway
- **Sill**: Bottom of window opening
- **Header**: Top of door/window opening

### Spatial

- **Double-height**: Space extending two floors
- **Mezzanine**: Intermediate floor level
- **Atrium**: Central open space
- **Alcove**: Recessed area in wall

## Famous Buildings Reference

When generating tactile graphics of well-known buildings:

### Modernist

- **Villa Savoye** (Le Corbusier): Pilotis, ramp, roof garden
- **Farnsworth House** (Mies): Single room, glass walls
- **Fallingwater** (Wright): Cantilevers over waterfall

### Contemporary

- **Guggenheim Bilbao** (Gehry): Curved titanium forms
- **Jewish Museum Berlin** (Libeskind): Angular voids
- **CCTV Building** (OMA): Continuous loop tower

### Classical

- **Pantheon**: Circular temple, oculus
- **Parthenon**: Columned temple
- **Notre-Dame**: Gothic cathedral, flying buttresses
